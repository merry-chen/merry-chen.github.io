Burnaby

Central Park

This 86.4-hectare urban park is notable for its immense douglas fir, western hemlock, cedar, poplar and maple groves. Central Park is known for its excellent sports and recreation facilities. Tennis courts outdoor swimming pool, horseshoe pitch and a pitch-and-putt golf course are popular draws. Anchoring the park is Swangard Stadium, host facility for many sporting, cultural, corporate and fundraising events.

Start in the parking area off Boundary Road. This park has many trails and it is easy to get lost, so stop at the kiosk and familiarize yourself with the map. Follow the Terry Fox markers for an enjoyable walk that weaves around the park.

Large towering evergreen trees create the forest canopy up to 60 metres above you. Evergreens are referred to as conifers because their seeds are usually found in cones. Cedars, in particular, are culturally important evergreens and are often referred to as the “tree of life” because they provided aboriginal people with shelter, clothing, tools and transportation.
