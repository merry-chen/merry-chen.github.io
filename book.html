About

In my Media Across Cultures course, we had to create a new media project centered on the theme of cultural hybridization. In seven weeks, our team created an educational children’s book to give the readers a better awareness and understanding of child labour and globalization. Flowers for the Boy provides young readers with a unique learning experience and perspective about the realities that children face on the other side of the world.

Skills

Research, Concept Development, Writing and Editing, Book Layout and Design

Tools

Illustrator

Research Component

Objective

Our objective for Flowers for the Boy is to design a learning experience for children in western society about child labour and globalization. Our targeted age group is between eight and twelve because we want to build a foundation of this global issue in their minds early on to set a deep impression on the harsh reality that happens to children on the other side of the world.

Even though the readers have not experienced life as a child labourer, they can try to identify and relate to the child in the story as both are living out their childhood and have their own challenges, hopes, and dreams. We hope readers will enjoy the story and illustrations, but more importantly, it will leave an imprint on them. And as children are exposed to this issue when young, they are more likely to inspire change and make a difference in the world.

Conceptual Approach

We approach this project with the maturity and perspective of both a child and an educator. As a child, we want to understand how we learn, in what circumstances do we want to learn, and how we can apply what we have learned into our lives. And as an educator, we want to explore effective teaching methods to engage and stimulate a child’s mind to help them understand and enjoy what they learn.

Issues

Within child labour and globalization, we concentrate on the cause and and effect of child labour, the role of the cocoa production industry, and western consumerist society. Our story captures a child’s point of view as a child labourer in Cote d'Ivoire, a country in West Africa. Our story addresses a serious issue that expresses a degree of sadness, which might be difficult for young readers to comprehend. Therefore, in our story, the child is hopeful of the future; even though in reality, the child has a dim future.

Process

Week 1-2: Exploration and Research

Our team explored ideas and conducted in-depth research on child labour and globalization. We researched specifically on the cocoa system, cocoa production industry, economics, effects on children, how to write a children’s book (language, tone, and visual style that appeals to a child in western society), and the specific memes associated with child labour (consumerism, disparities between rich and poor). We combined our research into a pool of understanding.

Week 3-4: Write Book

We brainstormed and discussed potential ideas for a story. Once we had established a strong concept, we constructed and wrote our narrative.

Week 5-6: Illustrate and Design Book

With the story completed, we created a storyboard that outlined all the scenes in the narrative. This helped identify important events in the story for us to illustrate. Once we selected the scenes, we created hand-drawn sketches. Once satisfied with the drawings, they were brought into Adobe Illustrator in which we polished them into detailed illustrations. Along with the illustrations, we had to design a book jacket cover and the book layout.

Week 7: Edit and Print Book

The book needed to be finalized and ready to print. We continued to revise, edit, and proofread our writing.
