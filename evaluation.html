DDR Field Study

Introduction

Our team of four conducted a field study to evaluate people’s emotional and affective experiences using a full body video game, Dance Dance Revolution. To gather this information, we used the Intrinsic Motivation Inventory (IMI) Post-Play Questionnaire and Cued Recall Debrief (CRD). The collected data helped to guide and support our controller mappings and screen layout redesign goals. The evaluation was conducted with four participants. Each session lasted approximately half an hour. Once the participant finished the play session, we had them complete the questionnaire and debrief.

IMI/Enjoyment Evaluation Method

The IMI/Enjoyment questionnaire measured the participant’s interest and enjoyment of the game. Participants were asked to respond to a series of twenty-two statements on a likert scale. For each of the following statements, the participant indicated how true it was for them, using the following scale: 1 (not at all true) to 4 (somewhat true) to 7 (very true).

Cued Recall Debrief (CRD) Evaluation Method

The CRD measured the affect of the participants towards the game. During the debriefing, we showed each participant the video of them playing the game in first person (shows the game screen) and third person (shows their body movement) point of view. Using prompts and think aloud, the participants recalled their affective states experienced during the game.

Data Analysis

Two evaluators reviewed the play sessions recordings, first individually, and then together. To review and evaluate the debriefing session, I listened for affective words and expressions. Using a data sheet, I recorded their comments, identified them as positive / negative / neutral, and grouped similar responses. Afterwards, the two evaluators came together to compare results and calculate the inter-rater reliability value to determine discrepancies.

Design Considerations

As a team, we reviewed the compiled data and brainstormed redesign improvements for the controller mappings and screen layout.

BC Hydro Heuristic Evaluation

Introduction

This assignment was completed for a fourth-year design evaluation course. Our team of three completed a heuristic evaluation of the BC Hydro website. This assignment received a 90% and was selected by the professor to be given to the marketing and advertising department at BC Hydro PowerSmart.

Individual Inspections

We explored the BCHydro website and found five critical tasks customers would typically perform on the site. We used Jakob Nielsen’s usability heuristics to guide our evaluation. Each team member conducted the evaluation individually. Using a data sheet, I noted the heuristic violated and described the usability defect. I ran through the five tasks twice. The first run allowed me to familiarize with the interface and tasks. And the second run was to use the heuristics to look for problems. View the Inspection Data Sheet.

Team Summary of Problems and Recommendations

As a team, we reviewed each other’s findings, assigned severity ratings to them, and brainstormed recommendations for each usability issue. We compiled our findings and improvements into a report. I was in charge of writing the scenario, methodology, and results and interpretation. Read the Heuristic Evaluation Report.
