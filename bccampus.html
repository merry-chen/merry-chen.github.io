Research Assistant

BCcampus (Co-op)

About

BCcampus is a publically-funded organization that utilizes information technology to provide technologies and services to learners, educators, and administrators of all post-secondary institutions in British Columbia.

I was hired as a Learner Services Research Assistant on the ApplyBC team. ApplyBC is a centralized, online application service that allows anyone to apply to a public B.C. post-secondary institution. More than 100,000 prospective students use ApplyBC each year.

During my 8-month co-op term, I modified the html/css of reference pages, performed functional and quality assurance testing, conducted a usability study, and executed an online survey of the ApplyBC website.

Skills

Research and Writing, Website Testing, Quality Assurance, A/B Testing, Heuristic Evaluation, Usability Testing, and Online Surveys.

Tools

Illustrator, Excel, Screen Capture, Lime Survey

Team

Dave Dumaresq, Patricia Bernal, Gilbert Fung

Website Testing

Introduction

When I started, ApplyBC was approaching the website testing phase of its development site. During this time, I conducted functional and quality assurance testing by creating test cases, coordinating evaluators, and communicating results to the development team. My task was to ensure the redeveloped website compiled with web standards before being released into the production stage. Additionally, many of the post-secondary schools had their own branding (banner, marketing graphic, and colour). Branding and institution-specific content needed to be translated into their online application form, so I utilized Eclipse to modify the html and css of the ApplyBC reference pages for the developers.

Process

Prepare Tests:
Create personas, prepare test scenarios, prepare test script, and find evaluators.
Conduct Tests:
Perform functional test, comparison test, concurrent test, and break test.
Report Results:
Compile test results, extract distinct issues, organize bugs by severity, and report bugs to JIRA bug tracking system.
Conduct Tests:
Retest JIRA issues, and perform verification and validation tests.

Usability Study

Introduction

Once the redeveloped ApplyBC website launched, I conducted a usability study with five participants. The purpose of the usability study was to identify new usability problems, determine if specific goals and requirements have been achieved, and establish a baseline of usability metrics that can be used to compare and measure future releases. I helped design the test plan, recruit the participants, facilitate the test, and prepare the report of findings.

Read the Usability Study Report.

Process

Preparation:
Prepared research questions, methodology, procedure, user tasks, scenarios, test script, data-collection forms, and post-study debrief questions.
Recruitment:
Researched demographics, created user profiles, posted craigslist ad, screened participants, and sent confirmation emails and consent forms.
Usability Test:
Performed walkthrough and pilot test, sent reminder emails, set up test environment, and conducted tests.
Data Analysis:
Organized usability issues by location and severity, reviewed and marked test videos, and prepared findings and recommendations.

Online Survey

Introduction

Another project that I worked on was the ApplyBC and student services survey. BCcampus surveyed ApplyBC users to gather feedback on the online application service and the application process. The survey ran for 20 days and generated 821 responses. For this assignment, I created the survey questions, designed the survey, analyzed the data, and prepared the report. The survey results were used by the developers to improve the ApplyBC user experience.

Read the Online Survey Report.

Process

Preparation:
Researched survey methodology, created survey questions, and reviewed questions with manager.
Survey:
Created survey on LimeSurvey, composed survey email, and tested email campaign.
Data Analysis:
Compiled and calculated survey data into excel, organized and analyzed survey comments, created a summary report, charts, and infographics.

Learning Outcomes

At BCcampus, I enjoyed the autonomous work environment without direct supervision. Since there was no checklist of tasks to accomplish by the end of the day, I learned to be more self-motivated and disciplined. My supervisor, Dave always reminded us that our main responsibility as students is to learn. And because of this, I was able to dedicate my time to learning and developing my expertise, and applying my knowledge and skills into the workplace.
