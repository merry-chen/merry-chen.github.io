Team Xpress

Hybrid Mobile App

About

This project was created for an Information Design course. Our team of four created an application that provides university students a simple and quick way to check their health coverage, locate health networks, file a claim, and view their claim history. Designed for mobile and tablet devices, students can make the most of their benefits and save on their health-care.

Process

  • Conducted usability tests and refined prototype.

Use Cases

Short Survey

We conducted a short online survey, and collected 42 results from Simon Fraser University students. We asked them what kind of health services they had used in the past year, and the frequency of those services. From our data, we identified three groups of members: light, medium, and heavy.

Types of Members

Light members are occasional users of health services; a few times each year, mainly for medical emergencies. Medium members are average users of health services; every couple of months. Heavy members are frequent users of health services; once every week or couple of weeks.

Journey Framework

Scenario

In her calculus lecture, Julie squints to see the whiteboard, and thinks she needs stronger glasses. She visits an optometrist near her place, gets her eyes examined, and selects the frame and lens for her eyeglasses. Afterwards, she goes to the library to print the claim form, photocopy the receipts, and completes the form. She goes to the bank to get a void cheque in order to receive her payment by direct deposit. She goes to the post office to purchase a stamp, and mail the claim form. Three weeks later, she receives an email from the insurance company that her claim has been processed and the money has been deposited into her bank account.
