UI/UX Designer

SAP Germany (Co-op)

About

SAP provides enterprise application software to help companies run their business. Within SAP, I worked on assignments for SuccessFactors, an SAP Company. SuccessFactors provides cloud-based Human Capital Management (HCM) software.

Skills

Brainstorming, Prototyping, Human-Centered Design, User Interface, User Experience, User Research

Tools

Axure, PowerPoint

Role

As a UI/UX design intern on the SuccessFactors team, I worked on user interactions, workflows, and interfaces for various HCM solutions. During my internship, I used Axure to create functional prototypes that met design and business requirements. The high-fidelity prototypes were used by developers for software development and user researchers for usability testing and customer calls. My responsibilities and tasks consisted of:

  • Attending weekly meetings and design sessions.
  • Observing usability studies and customer calls to better understand usability issues.
  • Listening and understanding design and business requirements of the different stakeholders.
  • Designing interfaces and workflows within constraints and guidelines.
  • Presenting designs to supervisor, project managers, senior designers.
  • Noting issues that user researchers discovered during usability studies to refine interface.
  • Refining interface based on tests and colleagues’ feedback.

The prototypes I worked on consisted of Payroll, New Hire, Employment History, and Time Off applications. Unfortunately, the projects I have worked on during my internship are protected by NDAs so I can only provide images already available to the public.

Learning Outcomes

At SAP, I learned how to work on projects on an international scale and communicate in a multinational environment. I worked alongside my manager and colleagues that are experts in their fields of user experience and user research, which provided me a wealth of knowledge and resources to develop my design, communication, and thinking skills. When presenting my work, I was able to explain the reasoning behind my design decisions and welcome feedback. I learned to negotiate and compromise between points of views to propel the design forward.
